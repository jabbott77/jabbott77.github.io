Temple Information

The San Diego Temple is considered one of the most aestheically pleasing temples of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints. It was built with two main spires, but you can see four smaller spires at the base of each main spire.The structure is 169ft.(52 m) This temple was announced on April 7th, 1984 and dedicated on April 25, 1993 by Gordon B. Hinckley. The San Diego Temple is the 47th constructed and 45th operating temple of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints. Located near the La Jolla community and in the same area as the Mormon Battalion Historic Site in Old Town, San Diego. The temple was built on a 7.2 acre plot, has 4 ordinance rooms and 8 sealing rooms, and has total floor of 72,000 square feet(6,700 m^2).
